What we do

We activate health startups, connecting them with investors and clinical partners to close gaps in health care delivery in challenging health care environments locally and around the world.

Identify problems.

Alongside the Elson S. Floyd College of Medicine, we forge partnerships with hospitals and clinics in challenging health care environments across the state. On-site clinicians, including medical students, identify problems that clinics and hospitals face.

Understand the scope. Apply existing solutions.

We work with clinicians to identify problems that can be solved. If the right technology already exists, we help practitioners adopt it.

Innovate solutions within the university.

If the right technology does not exist, we consult with in-house university experts. A creative approach to solving the problem could lead to the launch of a new business.

Forge solutions through business partnerships.

Beyond existing solutions and university-led approaches, we work with start-up companies and innovators to forge solutions for challenging health care environments. We establish seed funds to invest in technology, and advance concepts into viable products by driving collaboration between innovators and clinicians during the development process. The result is new solutions that have vetted technology and business models; real-world use by patients, providers and payers; and a viable revenue stream.

Beyond business.

Entrepreneurship, innovation, and the forward-thinking aspects of health technology help shape the health care of tomorrow. We collaborate with faculty, students, community members, business leaders, entrepreneurs, and other colleges and universities inside and outside of the Elson S. Floyd College of Medicine through hackathons and other events.
